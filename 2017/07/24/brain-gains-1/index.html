Brain gains

文章选自2017/7/22的The Economist。 这里对文章进行生词释义和句子分析，仅供学习使用，欢迎交流。

导读： 时至今日， 教育手段依旧没有质的飞跃， 即使一些IT，多媒体手段被应用到教学当中。 学生们依然无法真正享受到因材施教的最佳效果。 但是在最近， 马克.扎克伯格， 比尔.盖茨等科技大佬， 正在积极支持类似于Mindspark一类软件的发展， 力图为学生们提供量身定做的学习计划， 做到因材施教， 充分发挥学生的潜能。

  • 脑力收益。 一些场景下， 也可以理解为人才引进

Together, technology and teachers can revamp schools

技术与教师可以一起改进学校

  • revamp(v.): 改组，改进
    e.g. revamp plans 修订计划

IN 1953 B.F. Skinner visited his daughter’s maths class. The Harvard psychologist found every pupil learning the same topic in the same way at the same speed. A few days later he built his first “teaching machine”, which let children tackle questions at their own pace. By the mid-1960s similar gizmos were being flogged by door-to-door salesmen. Within a few years, though, enthusiasm for them had fizzled out.

在1953年， B.F.Skinner 参观了他女儿的数学课。 这个哈佛的心理学家发现所有的学生都在以相同的进度相同的方式学习相同的主题。 几天之后， 他制造了他的第一台教育机， 用来让学生们根据自己的进度来解决不同的问题。 但推广计划最终流产。

  • tackle problem: 解决问题。 tackle 通常用于指解决难题
  • at one’s own pace: 按照各自的进度
  • gizmo(n.): 小玩意儿，小装置
  • flog(v.): 出售，卖掉
    e.g. They are trying to flog their house. 他们正试图卖掉房子。
  • door-to-door salesman: 上门推销员
  • fizzle out: 终成泡影， 虎头蛇尾， 最终失败
    e.g. The game fizzled out in the second half. 比赛到了下半场叫人失望

Since then education technology (edtech) has repeated the cycle of hype and flop, even as computers have reshaped almost every other part of life. One reason is the conservatism of teachers and their unions. But another is that the brain-stretching potential of edtech has remained unproven.

自从那之后， 教育科技已经经历了重复的起起落落， 即使计算机已经几乎改变了生活的其他部分。

  • edtech(n.): 教育科技
  • hype and flop: 炒作和失败, 起起落落
  • unproven(adj.): 未经证实的

Today, however, Skinner’s heirs are forcing the sceptics to think again (see page 16). Backed by billionaire techies such as Mark Zuckerberg and Bill Gates, schools around the world are using new software to “personalise” learning. This could help hundreds of millions of children stuck in dismal classes—but only if edtech boosters can resist the temptation to revive harmful ideas about how children learn. To succeed, edtech must be at the service of teaching, not the other way around.

时至今日， Skinner的后继者们正在迫使怀疑论者重新思索。 在科技巨富Mark Zuckerberg和Bill Gates的支持下， 世界上的一些学校正在使用新的软件来个性化学习。

  • heir(n.): 继承人
  • billionaire techie: 家财万贯的科技迷
  • dismal class: 沉闷的课堂
  • edtech booster: 教育科技的支持者
  • resist the temptation: 抵挡诱惑
  • the other way around: 从相反的方向

Pencils down
The conventional model of schooling emerged in Prussia in the 18th century. Alternatives have so far failed to teach as many children as efficiently. Classrooms, hierarchical year-groups, standardised curriculums and fixed timetables are still the norm for most of the world’s nearly 1.5bn schoolchildren.

传统的教学模式在18世纪的普鲁士出现。 可替代方法远远不能有效地对大量的学生施教。

  • standardised curriculums: 被标准化的课程

Too many do not reach their potential. In poor countries only a quarter of secondary schoolchildren acquire at least a basic knowledge of maths, reading and science. Even in the mostly rich countries of the OECD about 30% of teenagers fail to reach proficiency in at least one of these subjects.

很多人都不能发挥出他们的潜力。

  • reach one’s potential: 发挥出潜力
  • OECD: 经济合作与发展组织
  • reach proficiency: 达到熟练的水平

That share has remained almost unchanged over the past 15 years, during which billions have been spent on IT in schools. By 2012 there was one computer for every two pupils in several rich countries. Australia had more computers than pupils. Handled poorly, devices can distract. A Portuguese study from 2010 found that schools with slow broadband and a ban on sites such as YouTube had better results than high-tech ones.

那样的份额在过去的15年中始终保持不变， 在这些年中学校已经花费了数十亿的资金在IT建设上。

  • handled poorly: 处理不当
  • slow broadband: 慢网速

What matters is how edtech is used. One way it can help is through bespoke instruction. Ever since Philip II of Macedon hired Aristotle to prepare his son Alexander for Greatness, rich parents have paid for tutors. Reformers from São Paulo to Stockholm think that edtech can put individual attention within reach of all pupils. American schools are embracing the model most readily. A third of pupils are in a school district that has pledged to introduce “personalised, digital learning”. The methods of groups like Summit Public Schools, whose software was written for nothing by Facebook engineers, are being copied by hundreds of schools.

重要的是教育科技如何被使用。 一种有效的方法就是采取因材施教的方法。

  • bespoke instruction: 因材施教
  • individual attention: 个体关照
  • school district: 学区

In India, where about half of children leave primary school unable to read a simple text, the curriculum goes over many pupils’ heads. “Adaptive” software such as Mindspark can work out what a child knows and pose questions accordingly. A recent paper found that Indian children using Mindspark after school made some of the largest gains in maths and reading of any education study in poor countries.

在印度， 有一半的孩子在离开小学之后不能阅读简单的文本， 课程被孩子们忘光。

  • work out: 计算出
  • pose questions: 提出问题
