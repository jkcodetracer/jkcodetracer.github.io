CoolDown 经济学人阅读(7) - Modi's India(2)

Posted on 2017-07-05 | In English Learning |

文章选自2017/6/24的The Economist。 这里对文章进行生词释义和句子分析，仅供学习使用，欢迎交流。

Political conditions are about as propitious for reform as they are ever likely to be. Mr Modi’s government is the strongest in decades. It has a big majority in the lower house of parliament and is edging closer to control of the upper house, as well. It runs most big states. The opposition is hopeless.

莫迪政府是近几十年来最强大的, 政治环境极有利于改革。 绝对优势的下议院， 还有几乎被控制的上议院

  • propitious(adj.):极好的时机
  • be edging closer to: 逐步逼近,逐渐接近

There are economic tailwinds, too. India is a big importer of oil; the low price of late has been boosting growth by perhaps two percentage points a year. Ageing has long weighed on Western economies and is starting to sap China’s. India, by contrast, is still young. Over a quarter of the people joining the world’s workforce between now and 2025 will be Indian. And there is enormous scope for catch-up growth: India is the poorest of the world’s 20 biggest economies. By rights, it should be surpassing others’ growth rates for years.

经济发展也有顺风车。低价原油，人口红利，以及巨大的增长空间–印度是全球20大经济体中最贫穷的

  • economic tailwind: 经济顺风。 比喻经济形势好
  • of late: 近来
    e.g. I’ve been rather ill of late. 近来我身体不好
  • boost growth: 促进增长
  • Ageing: 人口老龄化
  • sap(v.): 消耗(精力)
  • catch-up growth: 赶超式的增长

Mr Modi, in short, is squandering a golden opportunity. Some apologists claim that he is waiting until he wins a majority in the upper house before taking on bigger reforms. If so, he has given no inkling of what he is planning. In fact, he has not even made clear that economic reform is his priority.

但是莫迪却在肆意挥霍这个良机。 有人为他辩护，认为他在等待赢下上议院后才开始他的大改革。但事实是，他甚至没有弄清楚，经济改革才是第一要务

  • squander(v.): 挥霍，浪费
    e.g.
      1. squander one’s talent 浪费才华
      1. squander opportunities 浪费机会
  • apologist(n.): 辩护者
  • inkling(n.): 略知，模糊的概念
    e.g. I have no inkling of his purpose until much later. 直到最近我才对他的意图略知一二

More a chauvinist than an economist

莫迪是一个激进的爱国主义者，而不是一个经济学家

  • chauvinist(n.): 沙文主义者

As prime minister, Mr Modi has been just as careful to court militant Hindus as jet-setting businessmen. His government recently created havoc in the booming beef-export business with onerous new rules on purchases of cattle, in deference to Hindus’ reverence for cows (see page 54). Yogi Adityanath, the man he selected to run Uttar Pradesh, is under investigation for inciting religious hatred and rioting, among other offences.

作为总统， 莫迪精心地迎合激进的印度教教徒和生活奢华的商人。 鉴于宗教信仰，干涉牛肉出口，对Yogi Adityanath进行的全面调查

  • court militant Hindus: 讨好激进的印度教教徒
  • jet-setting businessmen: 生活奢华的商人们
  • create havoc: 造成破坏
  • onerous(adj.): 繁重的，艰巨的。 这里形容法规繁杂
  • in deference to: 鉴于… 这里是说考虑到印度教对牛的尊敬
  • reverence for sb/sth: 对某人/某事的尊敬
  • incite religious hatred: 煽动宗教仇恨

The fear is that, if the economy falters, Mr Modi will try to maintain his popularity by stirring up communal tensions. That, after all, is how his Bharatiya Janata Party first propelled itself to government in the 1990s. Mr Modi himself was chief minister of Gujarat in 2002 when rioting there killed at least 1,000 people, most of them Muslims. To this day, he has never categorically condemned the massacre or apologised for failing to prevent it.

如果印度经济衰退，莫迪很可能激化各个社会团体之间的矛盾来维持他的人气。

  • the economy falters: 经济衰退。 这是个完整的句子， ‘falter’ 作为动词，说明一种虚弱的，不能继续高效率的状态
  • stir up: 激起(情绪)
  • communal tensions: 社会各团体之间紧张关系
  • after all: 终究
  • categorically(adv.): 绝对地，明确地

Under Mr. Modi, debate about public policy, and especially about communal relations, has atrophied (see page 21). Hindu nationalist thugs intimidate those who chide the government for straying from India’s secular tradition, or who advocate a less repressive approach to protests in Kashmir, India’s only state with a Muslim majority. One of the few media companies that dares to criticise the government has been raided by police on grounds that would not normally attract such heavy-handedness. Mr Modi himself has become the object of a sycophantic personality cult. The prime minister may intend all this as a way to keep winning elections. But it is not hard to imagine it going disastrously wrong.

莫迪时期的印度， 关于公共政治和社会团体之间关系的讨论达到了低谷。 印度教的国家主义恶棍们积极打压相关的人群

  • under(prep): 在…时期
  • communal relations: 公共团体之间的关系
  • atrophy(v.): 衰退，萎靡。 这里指影响力太小而不受重视
    e.g. Many hoped he would renew the country’s atrophied political system. 很多人都希望他能使该国萎靡的政治体系振作起来
  • nationalist thug: 国家主义恶棍
  • intimidate(v.): 恐吓
  • chide(v.): 指责。告诉某人其意见和行为并不受到赞同
  • stray from: 偏离，迷路，走失。 一般说明远离预期的地点
  • secular tradition: 世俗的传统。 ‘secular’ 强调与宗教无关的
  • repressive approach: 高压的手段
  • raid(v.): 突袭搜查
  • heavy-handedness: 暴行
  • intend(v.): 想要。有计划、决定的含义
  • the object of: …的对象
  • sycophantic(adj.): 阿谀奉承的
  • personality cult: 个人崇拜
  • disastrously(adv.): 灾难性地

Mr Modi’s admirers paint him as the man who at last unleashed India’s potential. In fact, he may go down in history for fluffing India’s best shot at rapid, sustained development. And the worries about a still darker outcome are growing.

莫迪的崇拜者把他描绘成了释放印度潜力的人。 但事实上，他很可能搞砸印度为快速，稳定发展做出的最大努力，并淹没在历史长河之中。

  • unleash potential: 释放潜力
  • fluff(v.): 搞砸…
  • best shot: 最大努力
    e.g. Once you are in the building, you take your best shot at winning that job. 一旦你进了公司大楼， 你就该用尽全力争取到这份工作
